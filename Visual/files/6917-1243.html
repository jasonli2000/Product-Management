SPINAL INJURY ACCELERATION MONITORING SYSTEM, COMPUTERIZED. AN
INTERCONNECTED GROUP OF ITEMS DESIGNED TO STUDY THE THREE DIMENSIONAL
ACCELERATIONS OF THE HEAD AND BODY OF VOLUNTEERS AND PATIENTS. ITS
PURPOSE IS TO DETERMINE THE AMOUNT OF MOVEMENT AND VIBRATION THAT THE
SPINAL INJURY PATIENTS ENDURE DURING TRANSPORTATION AND REHABILITATION.
IT ALSO SIGNAL AN ALARM IF THE SAFE LIMITS OF ACCELERATION ARE
EXCEEDED.
