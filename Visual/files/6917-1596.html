AVERSION THERAPY APPARATUS, ELECTRIC. AN ELECTRONIC APPARATUS DESIGNED
TO BE USED FOR BEHAVIOR MODIFICATION BY USE OF A MILD AVERSIVE STIMULUS
(ELECTRIC SHOCK). ITEM IS USED TO CHANGE UNDESIRABLE BEHAVIOR SUCH AS
HABITS, ANTISOCIAL BEHAVIOR, NERVOUS DISORDERS, UNDESIRABLE SEXUAL
BEHAVIOR, COMPULSIONS, ADDICTIONS, AND THE LIKE. ITEM IS USUALLY
EQUIPPED WITH ELECTRODE WHICH IS WORN ON THE PATIENT'S ARM OR LEG.
SHOCK CURRENTIS TRANSMITTED TO PATIENT FOR SHORT, MEDIUM, 
 OR LONG DURATIONS AS DETERMINED BY THERAPIST. ITEM MAY BE COMPLETE
WITH ELECTRODES, ELECTRODE LEADS, REMOTE CONTROL, BATTERIES, AND THE
LIKE.
