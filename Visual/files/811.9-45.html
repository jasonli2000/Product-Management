Name Value
NAME VA-POLYTRAUMA MARKER
PRINT NAME Combat Polytrauma Confirmation
DESCRIPTION 
This reminder was activated through an algorithm which identifies 
patients who may have experienced severe Polytrauma due to OEF/OIF combat 
related injuries.  By definition, Polytrauma occurs due to a single event 
with injuries to more than one physical region or organ system, one of 
which may be life threatening, and which results in physical, cognitive, 
psychological, or psychosocial impairments and functional disability.  
The following questions will serve as the clinical confirmation that the 
injuries experienced by this patient meet the definition of severe 
Polytrauma related to OEF/OIF combat.  
 
Health factors are collected for each of the yes/no responses to the 4 
questions.  A 'yes' response to all 4 questions is a positive.
 
HEALTH FACTORS:
POLYTRAUMA OEF/OIF - YES
POLYTRAUMA OEF/OIF - NO
POLYTRAUMA SINGLE EVENT - YES
POLYTRAUMA SINGLE EVENT - NO
POLYTRAUMA EVACUATION - YES
POLYTRAUMA EVACUATION - NO
POLYTRAUMA IMPAIRMENT HISTORY - YES
POLYTRAUMA IMPAIRMENT HISTORY - NO
 
Combinations of the following reminder terms and associated taxonomies
define which patients may have experienced polytrauma and therefore need
to be asked about those injuries.
 
VA-POLYTRAUMA AMPUTATION
VA-POLYTRAUMA AUDITORY
VA-POLYTRAUMA BRAIN INJURY
VA-POLYTRAUMA BURN
VA-POLYTRAUMA INPT REHAB
VA-POLYTRAUMA ORTHO
VA-POLYTRAUMA PTSD
VA-POLYTRAUMA SCI
VA-POLYTRAUMA VISION
VA-POLYTRAUMA WAR INJURY
 
The reminder is resolved by a negative response to questions 1-4 or a 
positive response to question 4.   The health factors that represent 
these responses are included in a reminder term:
