GENERAL COHORT FOUND TEXT 
\\
    There are now two pneumococcal vaccines: Pneumovax and Prevnar 13.  
Talk with your health care team to find out if you should receive one or
both of these vaccines.
 
    Many different types of bacteria and viruses can cause pneumonia.  
Pneumococcal vaccines will help to protect you against one of the most 
common types of bacteria.  These vaccines help prevent the pneumonia from
becoming severe and help to keep the bacteria from going into the blood
stream.
 
    There are two different pneumonia vaccines: POLYSACCHARIDE
(Pneumovax) and CONJUGATE (Prevnar 13).  Talk with your Primary care
(PACT) team about whether you should receive one or both of these
vaccines.    
 
    You may need a second dose of the Pneumovax vaccine depending on your
medical conditions and when you received the first pneumonia vaccine.  If
you need a second dose, it is usually administered at least 5 years after
the first dose.  
 
    Please call your primary care team (PACT) to find out how you can get 
your pneumonia Vaccine. If you already had a pneumonia vaccine, please
tell your Primary care team (PACT) which vaccine you had at your next
visit.  Please bring a written record from the place that gave you the 
vaccine to your next visit.
\\
