VA-HOMELESSNESS SCREENING (66)    REMINDER DEFINITION (811.9)

Name Value
NAME VA-HOMELESSNESS SCREENING
PRINT NAME Homelessness Screening
DESCRIPTION 
The Secretary of the Department of Veterans Affairs has made it a 
priority to eliminate homelessness in our Veteran population.
 
The objective of the VA-Homelessness Screening Clinical Reminder is to: 
(a) identify Veterans and their families who are at imminent risk of 
homelessness or who have very recently become homeless; (b) ensure that 
those who are at-risk or who are currently homeless are referred for the
appropriate assistance; and (c) update the current living situation for
Veterans. Information collected by the VA-Homelessness Screening reminder
will guide staff to refer Veterans for further assessment and the
appropriate intervention to prevent or quickly end homelessness.
TECHNICAL DESCRIPTION 
The reminder applies to all Veterans, EXCEPT any Veteran where one of
the following findings in the Reminder Term (Finding #1) apply: 
1. Those who have had a visit in one of the specific Stop Code Clinics
(Homelessness-related) listed in the Reminder Location List ; or 
 
2. Those who have had the Health Factor, ALREADY RECEIVING ASSIST WITH
HOUSING entered into a visit by using the corresponding option in the
dialog; or 
 
3. Those that have had a Progress Note written with a SPECIFIC Title (that
is already entered into the logic of the Computed Finding [VA-PROGRESS
NOTE]) by a provider in a NON-Homelessness Stop Code clinic, who provided
those services.
 
Entering a Beginning Date/Time of T-6M on that Reminder Term is a logic
trick to ensure that only visits in those locations, or the Specific
Health Factor, or the specific Progress Note Title IN THE PAST 6 MONTHS
are evaluated.
 
This reminder's frequency is set to 1 year, but this required somewhat
complex resolution logic so that various findings and conditions resolve
the reminder for different time frames.
 
Negative Screens result in resolution for 6 Months. This was accomplished 
by creating a Function Finding - FF(#1), that sets the frequency.
 
 ---- Begin: FF(1) ----------------------------------------------
              Function String: MAX_DATE(3,5)'<MAX_DATE(2,4) 
     Expanded Function String: 
      MAX_DATE(POSITIVE - HAS WORRIES ABOUT HOUSING, 
      POSITIVE - HAS NO STABLE HOUSING)'<MAX_DATE(
      NEGATIVE - HAS STABLE HOUSING,NEGATIVE - HAS NO HOUSING CONCERNS)
          Match Frequency/Age: 6 months for all ages 
                   Found Text: Positive Screen more recent than Negative
                   Screen. Frequency changed to 6M.
 ---- End: FF(1) ----------------------------------------------
 
Finding #9 - NURSING HOME RESIDENT - resolves the reminder for 2 years 
(Frequency set on that finding, but Beginning Date/Time set to T-2Y, so 
that after 2 years, the finding no longer applies, and normal frequency 
(1Y) applies), until and if that living arrangement is documented again.
 
Findings #2 & #4 - NEGATIVE - HAS STABLE HOUSING and NEGATIVE - HAS NO 
HOUSING CONCERNS - are BOTH Required for Negative Screen. If BOTH are
found, the reminder is resolved for 1 year, but if 3 consecutive screens
are negative, and the Computed Finding that looks at the Reminder
Definition VA-HOMELESSNESS FREQUENCY 2Y is evaluated as TRUE, it is
resolved for 2 years.
 
A Positive Screen (Finding #5 - POSITIVE - HAS NO STABLE HOUSING; or
Finding #3 -POSITIVE - HAS WORRIES ABOUT HOUSING ) resolves for 6 months,
as does documenting UNABLE TO PERFORM HOMELESS SCREEN (Finding #7).
 
Finding #6 (Declines Homeless Screen) Resolves for 1 year.
                                 
Customized RESOLUTION LOGIC defines findings that resolve the Reminder:
 FI(9)!(FI(2)&FI(4))!FI(6)!FI(3)!FI(5)!FI(7)
 
Expanded Resolution Logic:
 FI(NURSING HOME RESIDENT)!(FI(NEGATIVE - HAS STABLE HOUSING)&
 FI(NEGATIVE - HAS NO HOUSING CONCERNS))!FI(DECLINES HOMELESS SCREEN)!
 FI(POSITIVE - HAS WORRIES ABOUT HOUSING)!
 FI(POSITIVE - HAS NO STABLE HOUSING)!
 FI(UNABLE TO PERFORM HOMELESS SCREEN)
