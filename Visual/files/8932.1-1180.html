DEFINITION 
Psychoanalysis is a comprehensive, theoretical framework which, when 
applied to a treatment process, consists of an intensive verbal, 
therapeutic relationship between an analyst and an analysand which aims 
for symptom relief, emotional growth, and personal integration. The 
psychoanalytic treatment process includes, but is not limited to, the 
recognition of unconscious processes and conflicts; the significance of 
developmental influences; and the impact of resistances, defenses, 
transference, and counter transference phenomena. Treatment is enhanced by 
an understanding developed in the analyst's training and personal 
analysis of unconscious manifestations, such as dreams, slips of the 
tongue, fantasies and day dreams. Psychoanalytic technique varies in 
relation to theoretical orientation.
