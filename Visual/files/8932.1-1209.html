DEFINITION 
Surgeons who can diagnose, treat, and manage children's urinary and 
genital problems. A pediatric urologist devotes a minimum of 50% of his 
or her practice to the urologic problems of infants, children, and 
adolescents.  Pediatric urologists generally provide the following 
services:  the evaluation and management of voiding disorders; 
vesicoureteral reflux, and urinary tract infections that require surgery; 
surgical reconstruction of the urinary tract (kidneys, ureters, and 
bladder) including genital abnormalities, hypospadias, and intersex 
conditions; surgery for groin conditions in childhood and adolescence 
(undescended testes, hydrocele/hernia, varicocele).
