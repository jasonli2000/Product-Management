NAME XUDOC DEVICE PROMPT QUEUING
HEADER QUEUING OUTPUT
TEXT 
 
There are two ways to invoke queuing at the device prompt.  You may either
respond with Q, then specify a device as follows:
 
     DEVICE: Q
     DEVICE: DVNM5
 
Or you may first specify a device, and then indicate that you would like to
have your output queued.  For example:
 
     DEVICE: DVNM5
     DO YOU WANT YOUR OUTPUT QUEUED? NO// YES
 
In both cases, you will then be asked to specify a time for queuing.  You
may accept the default (now) or indicate a later time in the usual format.
Queuing sends your output to the Task Manager for processing.  Meanwhile,
you may continue working without delay.  TaskMan will process your job in
the background and send it to the device you had requested.  (Freeing the
home device is not always possible since it involves detaching the home
device from the existing process, a procedure which is not supported by all
operating systems.)
