Name Value
NAME XQ-USER-SIGNON
HEADER SIGNING ON TO THE SYSTEM
TEXT 
In order to prevent unauthorized use of the computer system, each user
is assigned codes allowing access.
 
On entering the system, you will be prompted for an ACCESS CODE, then
a VERIFY CODE.  These codes are initially assigned by the site manager.
On recieving your codes, do not write them down, and never share them
with another user.  Entering these codes will identify you to the computer,
and your name will be associated with many of the functions you perform
thru the computer. (ie. Sending messages, Entering an RX, Registering a
patient).  When you sign-on, you may see a message stating the last time
you were on.  If you suspect someone is using your codes, it is important
that they be changed.
 
You can change your own VERIFY CODE thru the [COMMON MENU] option,
[EDIT USER CHARACTERISTCS].  You may be required to change your VERIFY
code periodically.  Your site manager can change your ACCESS CODE.
 
ACCESS and VERIFY can be entered on one line separated with a ';' to
speed up the sign-on process.
