An initialism or acronym, for example, JAMA or JPEN, if present,
may or may not be the official title of a journal; sometimes it
is not the official title even when it appears on the cover.  It
is difficult to predict which version of the title is the official
one.
 
If it seems likely that an initialism or acronym is the official
title of a journal, it is recommended that this be used as the first
search strategy, especially for current titles.  Initialisms and
acronyms are not often found on older journals; they are a fairly
recent trend.
 
If the initialism/acronym is not the official title but is prominent
on the cover, it will be present in the SYNONYM field, which has
been cross-indexed so that it will find the correct official title.
 
Variant spellings, including punctuation variations, and short forms
of a title have also been used as SYNONYMS.
