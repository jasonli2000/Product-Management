Occasionally, you will encounter some areas of confusion in selecting the
correct title from the Title Authority File (TAF) for the local serials
database:
  
1.  An [INITIALISM] or acronym, if present, may or may not be the official
title of a journal; sometimes it is not the official title even when
it appears on the cover.  It is difficult to predict which version of
the title is the official one.
  
2.  The same exact title may appear in the database [TWICE], 
either because (1) it is both the old and new name of a journal (and a
different name was used for that journal in the intervening years),
or (2) two different publishers may have used the same journal name.
There may also be a version of the title for the supplement as a 
separate title entry.
  
3.  Use of [PUNCTUATION] and the [AMPERSAND].
  
***  If the wrong title is selected, use the Remove Serial Title
From Database [RST] option immediately to remove it.
